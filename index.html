Kitchen prep
~35 mins
✓
Cook & shred chicken breast (4–5 portions)
EASY ↑
Season with garlic powder, salt, pepper. Bake at 200°C for 22 mins or poach. Shred with two forks. Portion into 5 small containers — one per day.
Covers: frittata all week + any high-protein lunch add-on.
✓
Make big batch shakshuka turkey sauce
EASY ↑
Brown 400g turkey mince. Add 400ml passata, garlic, cumin, smoked paprika, chilli flakes, salt. Simmer 10 mins. Divide into 4 containers.
Covers: 4 shakshuka breakfasts. Morning effort = reheat + crack 2 eggs. Done in 6 mins.
✓
Make big batch British beans & veg base
EASY ↑
Cannellini beans + passata + heaps of spinach, courgette, mushrooms, peppers, garlic. Season with smoked paprika, Worcester sauce, ACV. Simmer 15 mins. Portion into 4–5 containers.
Your highest fibre breakfast — most filling, best for blood sugar stability all morning.
✓
Make protein cookie dough batch
EASY ↑
Chocolate protein powder + PB2 paste + oat flour + oat milk + pinch salt + dark choc chips. Mix, roll into balls, refrigerate in a container.
Your 3pm binge buffer. Making it now means it's there before the craving — not after.
✓
Mix dry snack jar (PB2 + psyllium + cinnamon)
EASY ↑
5 tbsp PB2 + 5 tsp psyllium husk + 2 tsp cinnamon + tiny pinch salt. Mix in a small jar. Label it "3pm snack mix".
One scoop into yoghurt each day. Zero decisions, zero measuring, one minute to make your snack.

Fridge setup
~15 mins
✓
Halve cherry tomatoes & store in container
EASY ↑
One container covers the full week of frittatas. Grab a handful each morning.
✓
Crumble feta into a jar
EASY ↑
Pre-crumbled and ready to scatter. Saves you tearing open a packet every morning.
✓
Slice spring onions & portion smoked salmon
EASY ↑
Slice the whole bunch of spring onions at once. Divide smoked salmon into daily portions — clingfilm wrapped or in small bags.
✓
Portion daily Greek yoghurt containers
EASY ↑
200g per container × 5. Stack in fridge at eye level — right at the front. Your 3pm snack is literally the first thing you see.
Eye level = grab first. This is environment design, not willpower.
✓
Label & arrange all containers front-to-back
EASY ↑
Monday → front. Friday → back. You always grab the front container — no thinking, no choosing.
Sounds fussy. Saves a decision every single morning for 5 days.
✓
Freeze raspberries (top up if needed)
EASY ↑
Keep a bag of frozen raspberries stocked. They go straight from freezer to yoghurt bowl — no thawing needed, creates a natural jammy swirl.
✓
Fill 3 water bottles & put in fridge
EASY ↑
Cold water is easier to drink than warm. Having it ready removes the "I'll drink more water" intention-to-action gap. 2.5L daily target = roughly 3 standard bottles.

Remove friction — wrong things harder
~10 mins
✓
Remove biscuits & chocolate from desk area
HARD ↑
Move to a high shelf, a different room, or don't buy them this week. Out of sight dramatically reduces impulsive eating — the craving needs proximity to act on it.
Don't rely on willpower. Rely on distance. If it takes 10 steps to get, you'll often not bother.
✓
Move sweet snacks to highest shelf or out of eyeline
HARD ↑
If others in the house buy sweet food, ask them to keep it in a specific cupboard you don't open. You don't need to ban it — just make it require a deliberate choice, not a reflexive grab.
✓
Delete or move food delivery apps off your home screen
HARD ↑
Move Uber Eats, Deliveroo etc to a folder on the last screen. One extra tap is often enough friction to stop an impulsive order during an emotional moment.
Don't delete — just relocate. The goal is a pause, not a ban.
✓
Put your 3pm snack at eye level — front of fridge
HARD ↑
Make the right choice the obvious choice. When you open the fridge at 3pm, the first thing you see is your yoghurt bowl, not something you'll regret.

Gym & movement setup
~5 mins
✓
Put walking shoes by your desk (not in another room)
EASY ↑
The #1 reason people skip the break walk is not laziness — it's friction. Shoes already on your feet means the walk starts the moment you stand up.
✓
Pack gym bag & leave it by the door
EASY ↑
Packed bag = no decision barrier on gym days. Decide tonight which 3 days this week you're training and pack accordingly.
✓
Choose your walk podcast or playlist for the week
EASY ↑
Pick one show or playlist that's only for walking. Download episodes for offline. The anticipation of hearing it becomes part of the walk motivation.
Temptation bundling — you'll start looking forward to the walk because of what comes with it.
✓
Schedule 3 gym sessions in your calendar
EASY ↑
Treat them like appointments. Decide now — don't leave it to "I'll go when I feel like it." Any 3 days work, just commit on Sunday.

Mind & week setup
~10 mins
✓
Review last week — one win, one lesson
MIND
Not a full debrief. Just: what went well this week? What would I do differently? Write it down somewhere, even a voice note. Reflection without self-criticism.
Looking at your scan trend counts as a win check — you're already moving in the right direction.
✓
Identify this week's most stressful day
MIND
Which day is most likely to trigger the binge? Plan now: have extra snacks prepped that day, schedule the 2pm walk as a hard block, and have a "if I feel overwhelmed I will..." statement ready.
Emotion eating is predictable when you look ahead. Plan the hard day before it arrives.
✓
Set a bedtime alarm for this week
MIND
Not a wake-up alarm — a "start winding down" alarm. 30 mins before your target sleep time. Sleep is when lean mass is built and cortisol resets. Poor sleep makes the 3pm craving much worse.
✓
Take this week's progress measurement
MIND
Waist measurement or progress photo — same time, same conditions. Don't rely on the scale alone. Your scan showed 44.5kg lean mass to protect — track what matters.
✓
Write your intention for the week (one sentence)
MIND
Not a goal list — one sentence. "This week I'm focusing on hitting my protein and doing both walks every day." Put it somewhere you'll see it: phone wallpaper, sticky note, wherever.
Specificity beats ambition. One clear focus beats five vague ones.
